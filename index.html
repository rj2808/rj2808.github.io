Hello, I am Rakshit, an Applied Physics graduate student at Cornell.

I am interested in solid state physics. I work under the guidance of Dan Ralph in the physics department. Recently, I have been working on the interplay of charge and spin and heat in exotic quantum materials. My work has lead to detection of magnetic spin Hall effect in collinear antiferromagnets, spin Nernst effect in topological insulators and heavy metals. In the previous part of my life, I worked on multiple projects on radical pair mechanism of avian magnetoreception, NV centers in diamond. For which, I have been fortunate to work with Vishvendra Poonia.


Apart from academics, I am interested in hiking (I completed Mountain Adventure Course at JIMWS), running, climbing and outreach, playing multiple musical instruments and dancing with fellow Tangueros.
